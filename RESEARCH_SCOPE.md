# Research Scope

## Objective

Open Crypto Factor Research evaluates whether cryptocurrency cross-sectional
return relations remain statistically credible and economically useful after
realistic data, multiplicity, cost, and prospective-evidence constraints.

The economic objective is a robust net benefit after fees, spread, impact,
funding, capacity, drawdown, and operational failure. The repository does not
assume that a historically significant coefficient is a deployable strategy.

## Unit of Research

The primary unit is a **factor hypothesis**, not a complete trading strategy.
A factor maps information available at a declared timestamp into a
cross-sectional ranking or exposure. Portfolio combination, paper trading, and
capital allocation are later and separate research stages.

## Core Questions

1. **Replication:** Does a published or mechanism-based relation survive an
   explicit adaptation to the available cryptocurrency panel?
2. **Identity:** Is the measured effect distinct from market, size, momentum,
   liquidity, and other declared baselines?
3. **Economics:** Does the effect survive turnover, fees, slippage, funding,
   borrow, liquidity, capacity, and stress assumptions?
4. **Robustness:** Is the evidence stable across time windows, liquid subsets,
   liquidity buckets, and adverse market regimes?
5. **Multiplicity:** Is the evidence still credible after every attempted
   candidate, including failures and invalid candidates, is counted?
6. **External validity:** Does a frozen rule accumulate useful evidence on
   observations that occur strictly after its research contract is frozen?

## Current Empirical Domain

- **Market:** cryptocurrency spot and perpetual markets, with current public
  acquisition centered on OKX.
- **Cross-section:** 50 registered assets and point-in-time liquidity
  eligibility capped at 40 assets in the current substrate.
- **History:** interfaces and audits for a 730-day panel. Extending to three to
  five years is an open objective where source history permits.
- **Fields:** OHLCV, quote volume, sparse realized funding, spot-perpetual basis,
  open interest, market capitalization, listing age, and broad asset labels.
- **Horizons:** primarily daily, weekly, and monthly factor research. Horizon
  choice must follow a source or economic mechanism, not a free parameter grid.
- **Families:** market, size, momentum, liquidity, reversal, low volatility,
  basis, funding, open-interest crowding, and mechanism-based combinations.

## Evidence Contract

An admissible hypothesis declares its source, economic mechanism, required
data, formula family, expected direction, neutralization, portfolio policy,
baselines, and failure conditions before evaluation. A frozen candidate ID
cannot be revised after its result is observed.

Candidate selection may use in-sample and validation evidence under the
declared policy. Holdout details do not return to candidate generation. A
historical clue can authorize only prospective observation. Promotion to
portfolio research requires a separate, frozen future-data policy.

## Role of AI

AI-assisted hypothesis generation can help read registered sources, express
mechanisms as standardized formulas, and produce bounded candidate batches.
AI is not the source of truth and does not judge its own candidates. It cannot
access sealed Holdout feedback, alter a frozen candidate, bypass a trial
budget, or authorize capital.

The central contribution is the evidence protocol. The validity of a result
must not depend on whether a candidate was proposed by AI or by a person.

## Out of Scope

- Claims of guaranteed or stable profit.
- High-frequency market making, latency arbitrage, or queue-position models.
- Unbounded formula search or parameter sweeps after viewing outcomes.
- Treating current-live instruments as a complete historical delisting panel.
- Filling missing basis, funding, returns, or pre-listing history with invented
  observations.
- Using a standalone perpetual-return sign to represent a two-leg basis trade.
- Treating a historical factor clue as a strategy, paper-trading pass, or live
  deployment decision.

## Claim Ceiling

The current historical sample is survivor-conditioned before the universe
freeze. It can reject hypotheses and identify bounded research clues, but it
cannot by itself establish a deployable factor. Formal external evidence must
come from immutable prospective observations or from a demonstrably complete
historical delisting archive.

## Preservation of Historical Names

Earlier internal modules and dated records use `BTCLab`, `factory`, and related
operational names. Renaming those files would alter paths and hashes cited by
existing audit artifacts. They remain unchanged as provenance, while the
public project identity and current research claims are defined by this file,
the README, and the evidence ledger.
