# Economic Alignment Self-Audit

Date: 2026-07-18

## Verdict

The project has not abandoned the economic path, but its written North Star
and two stale priority markers still overvalued factory hardening. A real
prospective-evidence incident also showed that the framework could protect
integrity while still failing to collect useful evidence.

The corrected ultimate goal is:

> Discover, combine, and eventually deploy a robust crypto strategy whose
> expected benefit remains positive after realistic fees, spread, impact,
> funding, capacity, drawdown, and operational failures.

AI generation, literature constraints, preregistration, historical audit, and
automation are instruments for reaching that economic goal. A factory that
only rejects candidates or runs reliably without producing economically useful
evidence is not a successful final product.

## What Remains Aligned

- No historical result currently authorizes capital, a combo, or a strategy.
- The sole 90-day low-volatility clue remains unpromoted and unchanged.
- Cost and horizon work is factor-neutral and belongs to every future factor.
- Holdout is not used to choose formulas, signs, costs, or horizons.
- Rejected funding, basis, and momentum paths have not been renamed or tuned
  from their failed outcomes.
- The trial registry still contains 140 events across 46 candidate IDs; only
  one candidate is a prospective clue and none is a promoted factor.
- All real AI sources remain closed, so the unattended scheduler idles instead
  of generating unsupported variants.

These controls agree with the registered prequential, data-snooping,
multiple-testing, transaction-cost, and crypto factor literature. They are
necessary, but they are not themselves evidence of profitability.

## Deviations And Blind Spots

### R0: Prospective evidence was not accumulating

The 2026-07-17 universe and data snapshots succeeded, but the first factor
snapshot failed its frozen contract. The v1 contract included unrelated shared
modules, so a candidate-schema change invalidated the track even though the
factor formula, 90-day window, return, costs, and promotion policy did not
change.

This failure correctly produced zero formal days, but leaving it unresolved
would have made a one-year wait economically useless. V1 is now permanently
invalidated with no backfill. V2 starts on 2026-07-18 under a semantic evaluator
contract that binds the low-volatility calculation and point-in-time universe
behavior rather than unrelated factor-factory code.

Canonical incident record:
`logs/prospective_track_contract_incident_20260718.json`.

### R1: Roadmap priorities contradicted the economic override

The roadmap still marked critic approval and formula-audit hardening as current
priorities even though the economic agenda had moved them off the critical
path. Those markers are now backlog items. They become urgent only if they can
manufacture a false economic conclusion or block an admitted supervised study.

### R1: The current L2 study can become another engineering detour

The frozen multi-regime L2 sample is justified because it produces a reusable
execution-cost surface and a yes/no source-admission decision. It is not an
open-ended order-book platform project. After the frozen sample is processed,
the project must freeze the cost surface and make the source decision. More L2
work requires a new economic question, not a desire for technical completeness.

### R1: Evidence scope remains materially narrower than the literature

The current historical panel is a survivor-conditioned set of 50 registered
OKX perpetual assets with about 730 days in the main substrate. The relevant
microstructure paper uses 565 active and inactive spot assets from more than
250 venues over 2018-2024. We may study liquid OKX perpetual economics, but we
cannot claim to have replicated the broad crypto population or its illiquidity
premium.

## Binding Priority Order

1. Keep the v2 prospective track collecting unchanged future evidence.
2. Complete the already frozen multi-regime L2 sample once, freeze the shared
   cost surface, and decide whether the microstructure source opens.
3. If the source opens, evaluate only its canonical bid-ask and turnover-
   volatility paths. If it fails, close it without parameter mining.
4. Select the next literature mechanism only when the required data and an
   executable economic interpretation exist.
5. Expand history and active/delisted point-in-time breadth before making broad
   population claims.
6. Resume generic factory hardening only when it blocks valid economic evidence
   or when a real source is ready for unattended generation.

## Stop Conditions

- Do not call factory uptime, test count, or rejection count an economic result.
- Do not loosen a gate because no candidate passes.
- Do not let the 90-day clue choose the shared cost model or horizon.
- Do not run unlimited AI variants to force a winner.
- Do not spend more on the current L2 branch after its frozen decision is made.
- Do not place capital until promoted factors form a strategy that passes
  prospective, paper, execution, and risk gates.

## Plain Conclusion

The goal is understood. We are building a repeatable route to positive net
economic value, not a perfect research framework and not a machine that prints
backtest passes. The project is currently in economic discovery and evidence
collection, before factor combination, paper trading, or capital deployment.
